My Motivation

To be able to survive and pass on your genes to the next generation so that they too can be given a chance to compete in the natural race and dominate as you did - natural selection. At its core is a mechanism through which, plastic organisms in a shared environment can, in an ever-dynamic power structure, be born, grow, compete and reproduce in an increasingly complex and never-stagnating fashion.

It is through uncountable eons that the consequences of natural selection become blatantly apparent - the evolution of fangs, wings, poison, social awareness and intelligence; as notable examples.

The Darwinian framework for the study of the temporal evolution of an environment with many species in it poses an inspiration with which insight can be probed to better understand the underlying power dynamics of our society and how its constituents adapt. These dynamics are not apparent nor readily available to any observer - nor can they be patiently observed through any viable time-window.

Such insights can be used to qualitatively veer our attention to key areas previously relegated or outright ignored. A case study for this framework is the group consisting of 60 of the top-valued stock market shares belonging to two neighboring countries, Mexico and the USA.

These countries provide an environment in which companies can set up shop and be part of a scenario seen through macro-economical lenses. As time moves on, these companies experience highs and lows which impact either directly or indirectly the value of their own share value as well as others even if unbeknown to them. To identify these dynamics is my goal.

Progress

As the poster above shows, several numerically sound comparisons are made in the framework of classical information science - which quantify in the abstract how different, two objects are, in this case, such objects are graphs.

Several aspects are left to be considered - namely variations in the environment, the tracking of important geo-economical events and their correlation to movements in the stock market and of course, to consider a more comprehensible sample of shares.
